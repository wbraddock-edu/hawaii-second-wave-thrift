Our Story

Born from a love of Hanapepe, sustainability, and the magic of pre-loved things finding new homes.

Our Journey

From Garage Sale to Kauai's Beloved Thrift Shop

Second Wave Thrift started in 2014 when Kalani and Pua Kahananui began hosting community garage sales in their Hanapepe driveway. What began as a way to help neighbors clear out their homes grew into something much larger when they realized how much incredible stuff was being thrown away on Kauai — vintage aloha wear, beautiful furniture, rare books — all destined for the landfill.

In 2017, they opened a small storefront on Hanapepe Road, right in the heart of Hanapepe's famous gallery district. The location was perfect — tourists and art lovers who came for First Friday Art Night discovered Second Wave and became regulars. The shop became part of Hanapepe's cultural fabric.

Today, Second Wave diverts thousands of pounds of goods from Kauai's landfill each month, provides affordable goods to island families, and connects visitors with authentic pieces of island culture and history.
